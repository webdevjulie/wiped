Move In and Move Out Cleaning Pro Tips

With hundreds of move in and move out cleanings under our belts, we’ve seen it all and learned exactly what makes the process smooth, stress free, and spotless. Follow these pro tips to make your move seamless!

Quick Tips at a Glance:

  • calendar_today Schedule services on different days – Avoid overlap with movers, carpet cleaners, or other services.
  • check_circle Clean last – Make cleaning the final step before handing over the keys.
  • vpn_key Leave a key – Don’t wait around; let us access the home on time.
  • power Keep water and power on – Cleaning without utilities isn’t possible.
  • schedule Allow a few extra days – In case a re-cleaning is needed.

Schedule Services on Different Days

To get the best results, make sure other services like movers, carpet cleaners, or plumbers aren’t working at the same time as your cleaning. It’s hard to clean around moving boxes or contractors, and everyone works better with a little space.

Do the Cleaning Last

Cleaning should happen after everything else is done. Why? Because deposits, appearances, and overall quality can be affected if dust, dirt, or paint is tracked in after cleaning.

Ideal Move-Out Schedule:

  • Day 1: Pack and organize boxes.
  • Day 2: Movers come and remove all furniture.
  • Day 3: Repairs, patching holes, painting, and small projects.
  • Day 4: Home cleaning – this is when WIPED works our magic.
  • Day 5: Carpet cleaning (optional, for deep carpet care).
  • Day 6: Hand over the keys.

Leave a Key

Waiting around in an empty house is no one’s idea of fun. Leave a key in a safe spot under the mat, in a lockbox, or the mailbox so we can start promptly. This saves time and stress during your move.

Have Running Water and Power

Cleaning without water or electricity isn’t possible. Double check your utilities are on at least 24 hours before your scheduled cleaning to give the utility company time to activate.

Important: If utilities aren’t working when we arrive, the service will need to be rescheduled, which may include a cancellation fee.

Allow a Few Extra Days for Re-Cleaning

While rare, there are times a touch up may be needed or unexpected issues arise. Having access a few extra days after your scheduled cleaning ensures we can handle any last-minute needs without stress.

FAQs: Move-In and Move-Out Cleaning

Find answers to the most common questions about move in and move out cleaning services, deep cleans, and landlord expectations.

What’s the difference between a move-in and move-out cleaning and a regular cleaning?

keyboard_arrow_down
Move-in and move-out cleanings are more thorough than standard cleanings. They target hard-to-reach areas, deep-clean appliances, cabinets, closets, baseboards, blinds, and more making the home ready for the next resident.

How is a move-out clean different from a deep clean?

keyboard_arrow_down
Deep cleaning focuses on hard-to-reach areas and heavy buildup. Move-out cleaning includes everything in a deep clean plus extra attention to empty spaces, closets, drawers, and appliances for a fully spotless home.

How should I clean my home after moving out?

keyboard_arrow_down
  • Remove all personal items and trash.
  • Dust and wipe surfaces, shelves, and baseboards.
  • Clean appliances inside and out (oven, fridge, microwave).
  • Vacuum and mop floors.
  • Wipe windows, blinds, and light fixtures.

What’s included in a deep cleaning?

keyboard_arrow_down
  • Kitchen counters, sinks, cabinets, appliances, backsplash
  • Bathrooms: sinks, toilets, tubs, showers, mirrors, cabinets
  • Living spaces: dusting surfaces, baseboards, blinds, vents
  • Floors: vacuuming, mopping, carpet spot cleaning
  • Doors, frames, light switches, outlets
  • Interior windows and sills

Can a landlord charge for cleaning after I move out?

keyboard_arrow_down
Yes. Landlords can deduct cleaning costs from your security deposit if the home isn’t returned in reasonably clean condition.

Do I need to clean the oven when moving out?

keyboard_arrow_down
Yes. Most landlords expect the oven to be free of grease, food residue, and burnt-on stains.

How can I clean my home professionally, fast?

keyboard_arrow_down
  • Start with high impact areas: kitchen, bathrooms, floors
  • Gather all cleaning supplies beforehand
  • Work top-to-bottom: dust first, then clean floors
  • Use professional grade cleaners and tools for efficiency

What does a typical move-in/move-out cleaning include?

keyboard_arrow_down
  • Kitchen: counters, cabinets, appliances, sink, floors
  • Bathrooms: toilets, sinks, tubs, showers, mirrors, floors
  • Dusting: baseboards, blinds, vents, ceiling fans, light fixtures
  • Doors, frames, light switches, outlets
  • Windows: interior panes and sills
  • Closets, drawers, and other hidden areas

Do I need to be home during the cleaning?

keyboard_arrow_down
Not required. Many clients provide access via keys, lockboxes, or codes. Being home is optional but helpful for specific requests.

Can't Clean

Is there anything WIPED can’t clean? We get a lot of questions about certain tasks in regard to move-in and move-out cleanings. Because of this, we’ve felt compelled to add a few notes on what we can’t clean when it comes to a move-in/move-out cleaning.

Outside Windows

We don’t carry equipment and aren’t insured for cleaning the outside windows. If there’s dirt or grime on the outside it may leave the inside windows looking dirty after we’ve left. If you need help cleaning the outside windows, let us know. We’re always happy to refer to a business we know and trust.

Carpet Cleaning

We don’t carry equipment for steam cleaning or carpet stain removal. For serious carpet stains or if you’re in need of a steam cleaning, please contact a carpet cleaning specialist. If you’re looking for a referral, we’d be happy to provide one!
